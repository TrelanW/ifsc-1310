Welcome

NASA's Mars Rover Program represents one of the most ambitious and exciting chapters in space exploration. These robotic pioneers are designed to travel across the surface of Mars, conduct scientific research, and help answer fundamental questions about the Red Planet's history, climate, geology, and potential to support life. Since 1997, Mars rovers have helped transform our understanding of Mars. With each mission, NASA has introduced increasingly advanced robots‚Äîcapable of traveling longer distances, drilling into Martian rock, analyzing soil chemistry, and even flying in the thin atmosphere.

Why Mars?

Mars is the most Earth-like planet in our solar system. It once had flowing water, a thicker atmosphere, and possibly the conditions to support microbial life. By studying Mars, scientists gain insights not only into the planets past and present but also into Earths own history and future‚Äîand what it might take for humans to one day live on another world.
