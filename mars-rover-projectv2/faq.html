Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of NASA's Mars Rovers?

NASA's rovers are built to explore the surface of Mars, gather data on the planet's geology and atmosphere, search for signs of past life, and test technology for future human missions.

How do the rovers get to Mars?

Rovers are launched on rockets from Earth and travel millions of miles through space. They enter Mars' atmosphere and land using advanced systems like parachutes and sky cranes.

Why were Gale Crater and Jezero Crater chosen as landing sites?

How do rovers send information back to Earth?

Data is transmitted from the rover to satellites orbiting Mars, which then relay the information back to Earth via NASA's Deep Space Network.

How are Curiosity and Perseverance powered?

They use nuclear batteries called Multi-Mission Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators (MMRTGs), which provide long-lasting power and allow them to operate through dust storms and cold Martian nights.

What is the Ingenuity helicopter?

Ingenuity is a small drone launched with the Perseverance rover. It became the first aircraft to make powered, controlled flights on another planet.
