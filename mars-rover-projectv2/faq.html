Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of NASA's Mars Rovers?
NASA's rovers are built to explore Mars, study its climate and geology, and search for signs of past life.
How do the rovers get to Mars?
Rovers are launched aboard rockets and travel through space before entering Mars' atmosphere and landing with the help of complex systems like the sky crane.
Why were Gale and Jezero Craters selected as landing sites?
They both show evidence of ancient water, making them ideal for studying potential signs of past life.
How do rovers communicate with Earth?
They send signals to orbiters around Mars, which then relay the data back to NASA's Deep Space Network on Earth.
How are Curiosity and Perseverance powered?
They use nuclear batteries (MMRTGs), which provide long-lasting energy to operate through harsh Martian conditions.
What is the Ingenuity helicopter?
Ingenuity is a small drone that flew on Mars with Perseverance, performing the first controlled flights on another planet.
